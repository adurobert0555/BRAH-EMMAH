Dental Care

  • What type of toothbrush and toothpaste should I use?

    Soft-bristled toothbrushes are generally recommended, as they are gentle on gums and teeth.

  • Do I really need to floss?

    Yes, flossing is an essential part of oral hygiene. Flossing helps remove plaque and food particles that can get stuck between your teeth and under your gumline, where a toothbrush can't reach. It prevent gingivitis and periodontitis by removing plaque and bacteria that can cause inflammation and infection. Flossing can help remove trapped food particles and bacteria that can cause bad breath.

  • What are early signs of dental trouble?

    Gums that bleed easily when brushing or flossing. Persistent bad breath or a metallic taste in the mouth. Gums that are pulling away from the teeth. Pain or discomfort when biting or chewing. Visible holes or pits in the teeth. Yellow or brown spots on the teeth. Swollen, red, or tender gums. A dry, sticky feeling in the mouth. Changes in the way the teeth fit together when biting.

  • Why do I need dental test?

    X-rays can reveal issues like cavities, abscesses, or bone loss that aren't visible during a regular exam. Early detection of problems can prevent more severe issues, reducing the need for complex and costly treatments. Regular X-rays help track changes in your oral health over time.

  • How do fillings work?

    Fillings help restore the tooth's natural function and appearance. Fillings prevent bacteria from entering the tooth and causing further decay. Fillings can relieve pain and discomfort caused by tooth decay.

Eye Clinic

  • What do we do?

    We provide comprehensive eye care services, including: Routine eye exams, Vision correction, Eye disease diagnosis and treatment, etc.

  • What is Glaucoma?

    A group of eye conditions that damage the optic nerve, potentially leading to vision loss and blindness. It's a leading cause of irreversible blindness worldwide.

  • How does an EYE test work?

    You read letters or numbers from a chart (Snellen chart) to measure the sharpness of your vision. The eye doctor determines the correct lens prescription to correct nearsightedness, farsightedness, or astigmatism. The doctor examines the external and internal structures of your eyes, checking for signs of disease or abnormalities.

  • Why Regular Eye Tests Matter?

    Detect eye problems early, such as glaucoma or cataracts. Correct vision problems with glasses or contacts. Monitor eye health and prevent vision loss

  • What should I do to avoid eye irritation?

    Wash your hands frequently, especially before touching your eyes. Rubbing can irritate your eyes and spread germs. Wear protective eyewear when handling chemicals or cleaning products. Sharing makeup or makeup brushes can spread bacteria and cause eye irritation. Follow proper hygiene and removal techniques for contact lenses. Follow the 20-20-20 rule: every 20 minutes, look away from your screen and focus on something 20 feet away for 20 seconds. Regular eye exams can help detect and prevent eye problems.

Diagnosis

  • What do we know about diagnostic error?

    A diagnostic error occurs when a healthcare provider incorrectly diagnoses a patient's condition, leading to delayed or inappropriate treatment. This can result in harm, disability, or even death.

  • How often does it happen?

    Diagnostic errors are a pressing concern in healthcare systems worldwide, including in Ghana. These errors occur when healthcare providers are unable to accurately diagnose a patient's condition, leading to delayed or inappropriate treatment. The consequences of such errors can be severe, resulting in patient harm, increased healthcare costs, and a loss of trust in the healthcare system.

    In Ghana, diagnostic errors can be attributed to a combination of systemic issues, human factors, and limited resources. The country's healthcare system faces challenges such as inadequate infrastructure, a shortage of skilled healthcare professionals, and limited access to modern diagnostic equipment. These challenges can compromise the accuracy and timeliness of diagnoses, ultimately affecting patient outcomes.

  • What is the cause of diagnostic error?

    Diagnostic errors are a significant concern in healthcare, and understanding their causes is crucial for improving patient safety and outcomes. These errors can occur due to a complex interplay of factors, including cognitive biases, insufficient information, complex cases, human error, systemic issues, and limited access to care.

  • What are the obstacles to accurate diagnosis?

    Accurate diagnosis is a crucial aspect of healthcare, yet various obstacles can hinder the process. Understanding these challenges is essential for improving diagnostic accuracy and patient outcomes.

  • When is a formal diagnosis necessary?

    A formal diagnosis is a crucial aspect of healthcare, providing a comprehensive understanding of a patient's condition and guiding treatment decisions. It is essential for determining the underlying cause of symptoms, developing effective treatment plans, and monitoring patient progress.

    A formal diagnosis is necessary in various situations, including medical treatment, disability or insurance claims, mental health care, and medicolegal cases. It involves a comprehensive evaluation, including medical history, physical examination, laboratory tests, and imaging studies.

    By obtaining a formal diagnosis, healthcare providers can deliver high-quality care, improve patient outcomes, and ensure that patients receive the benefits and support they need. In conclusion, a formal diagnosis is a vital component of healthcare, enabling healthcare providers to make informed decisions and provide effective care.
